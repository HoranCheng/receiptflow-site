Getting Started

  1. Install the app from the App Store.
  2. Sign in with your Google account.
  3. Allow the requested Google Drive and Google Sheets permissions.
  4. Tap the scan button to photograph a receipt, or import one from Photos.
  5. Review any low-confidence receipts from the review queue.

Google Drive Permissions

ReceiptFlow needs Google Drive and Google Sheets access to store receipt images and maintain a receipt index spreadsheet. If you deny the requested scopes, sync and restore features will not work.

The app only creates and accesses files it has created — it cannot read or modify any other files in your Google Drive.

Restoring Receipts

After reinstalling the app, sign in with the same Google account. The app locates your existing receipt folder and receipt_index spreadsheet in Google Drive and restores matching receipt records to local storage.

If automatic restore does not find your data, go to Account → Advanced → Recover My Data and paste the link or ID of your receipt_index spreadsheet.

Deleting Data

Go to Account → Sign Out to remove local authentication state. To permanently delete all receipt data, go to Account → Advanced → Delete All Data. This removes local receipts, the app's Google Drive folder, and its spreadsheet.

You can also manually delete the ReceiptRenamer folder and receipt_index spreadsheet directly from Google Drive.
